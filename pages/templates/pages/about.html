About Foot and Mouth Disease

Understanding FMD and its impact on livestock

What is Foot and Mouth Disease?

Foot and Mouth Disease (FMD) is a highly contagious viral disease that affects cloven-hoofed animals, including cattle, pigs, sheep, goats, and other wildlife. It is characterized by fever and blister-like lesions on the tongue, mouth, feet, and teats.

While FMD rarely affects humans, it has severe economic consequences for the livestock industry. The disease spreads rapidly and can devastate animal populations if not properly controlled.

Signs and Symptoms

In Cattle

  • Fever (up to 41Â°C)
  • Blisters on tongue and mouth
  • Excessive drooling
  • Lameness
  • Reduced milk production
  • Weight loss

In Pigs

  • High fever
  • Blisters on snout and feet
  • Sudden lameness
  • Loss of appetite
  • Reluctance to move

How FMD Spreads

Direct Contact

Between infected and healthy animals

Airborne

Wind can carry the virus over long distances

Contaminated Items

Feed, equipment, vehicles, and clothing

Economic Impact

FMD has significant economic consequences for Eswatini:

  • Loss of international trade opportunities due to export bans
  • Reduced livestock productivity (milk, meat, draft power)
  • High costs of outbreak control and vaccination programs
  • Movement restrictions affecting farmers and markets
  • Loss of genetic resources from high-value breeding stock

Prevention is Key

Early detection and rapid response are crucial to controlling FMD outbreaks. If you suspect an outbreak:

Immediate Actions:

  1. Isolate affected animals immediately
  2. Contact Veterinary Services: +268 2404 2731
  3. Restrict all animal movements on and off your farm
  4. Implement strict biosecurity measures
  5. Keep detailed records of animal movements
